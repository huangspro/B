Li 2016

Methods

Double‐blind, randomised, non‐inferiority, multicentre clinical trial

Participants

232 participants (52.5% male) randomised to receive either ALC (N = 117) or methylcobalamin (N = 115). Participants with type 1 or type 2 diabetes mellitus were eligible if they were aged between 18 and 70 years (mean age 57.8 years), had been diagnosed with DPN according to electrodiagnostic criteria from the San Antonio Conference, and had abnormal nerve conduction velocity or amplitude, or both, in at least one nerve of the extremities.

Interventions

ALC 500 mg three times per day (N = 117) or methylcobalamin 0.5 mg three times per day (N = 115) orally for 24 weeks

Outcomes

Changes in the neuropathic symptom and sign scores from baseline to week 24, assessed by the Neuropathy Symptom Score (NSS)

Neuropathy Disability Score (NDS)

Change in the NCV and amplitude from baseline to week 24

Reversal rates of affected nerves at week 24

Safety endpoints included incidence and intensity of adverse events, and withdrawals due to adverse events

Funding sources

Liaoning Haisike Pharmaceutical Co.

Notes
